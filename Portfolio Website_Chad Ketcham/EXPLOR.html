Problem Statement:

People who love games, and more specifically scavenger hunt players, want an experience that is easy to use, fun and doesn’t hide good features behind a paywall.
Users want simple and intuitive interfaces. They also want to share the experience both in-person and on social media.

Interviews

After developing the problem statement I interviewed five people to better understand their perspective on mobile games, scavenger hunts and sharing experiences on social media.

Goals:

  • How people share experiences on social media
  • How important data usage and coverage is to the average user
  • Sites that should be included in a photo scavenger hunt
  • How people play games and if they pay for features

Insights:

  • Landmarks and iconic places were most important, second is local restaurants or places that you can only find in that city.
  • Social is important to most so having options for social integration is a must and some features such as stickers or geofilters could help make the experience better.
  • Tagging on social media is important to the experience for some.
  • The need to answer trivia to get clues to the next hunt item should at best be an option. Users seemed to just want a scavenger hunt and that’s all.

Personas

Using the insights gained from the User Interviews I put together three personas to represent the bulk of the target users. These helped direct my decisions throughout the entire design process.

Goals:

  • Create representatives of larger groups of users
  • Get a clear picture of users' needs, goals, and expectations
  • Find features that apply across all users
  • Get an empathetic foundation to make design decisions

User Journey

With the user personas in hand, I created a user journey for each of the personas. These gave me a starting point to test with users to see if my direction for the app was sound and how it could be refined.

Goals:

  • Find the most important features of the app
  • Prioritize aspects of the features most important to users
  • Test with users to see if my hypothesis' were correct
  • Help me understand users feelings and motivations
  • View the experience as a whole rather than a collection of features

Card Sorting

Choosing some of the main categories of the app I conducted a card sort to see if these made sense to the users or if there were categories they felt should be added and if the way I sorted the information was the most intuitive for them. I tested six participants and used a hybrid, online card sort.

Goals:

  • Test hypothesis for information architecture
  • Decide on the basic structure
  • Find labels for categories
  • Lockdown navigation features

Sketching

I began the design process by sketching options for the main screens of the scavenger hunt app.

Goals:

  • Iterate several options quickly
  • Easily combine ideas
  • Quickly discard ideas
  • Help visualize layouts rapidly

Wireframes

With an idea of the basic layout, I moved to wireframing. The wireframes were created using Adobe Illustrator and XD.

Goals:

  • Move sketches to the digital space
  • Make global design decisions clearer
  • Make choices on the priority of content based on space available
  • Begin connecting paths to screens

User Testing

After developing the wireframes they were then tested with users. I chose six participants that matched the user personas and used moderated in-person testing. The participants were then asked to complete two tasks on both mobile and desktop versions. They were also asked open-ended questions about their experience and asked to talk through their decisions as they made them.

Goals:

  • Learn if interactions are intuitive to the user
  • Find points of frustration
  • Get feedback about the user's feelings of the experience

User Testing Screens

High-Fidelity Prototypes

With the feedback from user testing, I created several rounds of mid-fidelity prototypes which I again tested with a group of users. Based on the feedback from those tests I was able to create high-fidelity prototypes using Adobe Photoshop, Illustrator and XD. These included colors, a more finished feel and most of the flow of the experience. These were shared with fellow designers to get feedback on the final design.

Goals:

  • To test hypothesis' about the flow from screen to screen
  • Test the overall visual appeal of the product
  • Refine and build out the look and feel of the UI
  • To check that the flow met users' needs

Accessibility Testing and Final Design

After most of the screens were created I checked the accessibility of the app against WCAG guidelines. In the places it was lacking I made improvements, particularly colors and font sizes. Also with feedback from other designers, I made changes to some of the features, navigation, and main screens. The feedback and fresh perspectives were invaluable. In order to build the design and visualize the interactions I used Adobe XD, Illustrator, Premiere Pro, After Effects, and Photoshop.

Goals:

  • Avoid low contrast colors
  • Make smaller type more legible
  • Enlarged buttons and rethink icons
  • Get feedback from other designers
  • Finalize this phase of the design

Design System

Finally, I built the design system using Adobe Illustrator, XD, and Photoshop.

Goals:

  • Maintain consistency as the app grows
  • Make building new screens more efficient
  • Allows quicker scaling
  • Keeps a steady record of all digital assets
